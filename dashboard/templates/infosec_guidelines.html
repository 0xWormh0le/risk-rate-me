Information Security Policies and Policy Control Technical Guidelines

Information security policies to safeguard a company’s information assets

These templates provide the guiding principles for sound information security management. It will help you establish controls to safeguard your company’s information assets against events that could compromise their confidentiality, integrity or availability. They will:

  • Enable a company to meet its responsibilities in terms of good corporate governance and compliance to all relevant internal and external standards;
  • Address a company’s security concerns and ensure that all staff will be aware of their responsibilities as producers and users of the information assets;
  • Protect the business by managing the risk of impact arising from failures of business continuity, unauthorised disclosure or distribution of confidential or sensitive information, or unauthorised changes to information;
  • Allow a company to react appropriately to reported information security incidents.
  • Provide Management support for the implementation of information security controls and alignment back to the company’s policies and framework for the co-ordination of information security initiatives;
  • Ensure Compliance with technology and industry specific standards.

The policy standards and procedures are a set of security best-practices necessary to prevent and detect the unauthorised access or misuse of information, whether intentional or accidental, and make an appropriate response and recovery if such access or misuse should occur.

The controls and polices in these documents can be used as a basis for tailoring further information security controls standards, deployment and procedures.
