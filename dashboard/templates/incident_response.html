Incident Response Readiness

Information security policies to safeguard a company’s information assets

Incident Response is a crucial part of the cyber security framework. Ensuring that you have the processes and structure in place to deal with any security incident will enable you to mitigate damages and recover quickly.

Security incidents are unavoidable. Preparations must be in place to quickly identify and respond to an incident. Whether it is a lost laptop or a malicious insider, organizations must be ready to respond. Having a plan available ahead of time is paramount to successfully managing the situation.

These resources will help you implement appropriate activities to take action regarding a detected cybersecurity incident. These documents should be tailored for you organisation and be regularly reviewed and updated.

Included are Incident Response and Disaster Recovery templates. The principal difference is their primary objectives. The purpose of an incident response plan is to protect sensitive data during a security breach, while a disaster recovery plan serves to ensure continuity of business processes after a service disruption. A good practice is to document two plans separately.

The example Incident Response Plans provide step by step instructional guides for handling a particular cyber-attack. Each playbook is a living document that must be regularly updated and reviewed.

Incident Response Plan

This document describes the overall plan for responding to information security incidents . It defines the roles and responsibilities of participants, characterization of incidents, relationships to other policies and procedures, and reporting requirements. The goal is to react to respond appropriately to the incident, communicate the results and risk to all stakeholders, and reduce the likelihood of the incident from recurring.

Disaster Recovery Plan

The purpose for this document is to inventory all of the IT infrastructure and capture all of the information required to facilitate the recovery from a disaster. It documents the steps that an organisation will follow in the event that a disaster occurs to restore network integrity and normal operations in a minimum time frame.

Business Continuity Plan

This policy provides guidance to assist personnel in performing their respective duties and responsibilities to ensure the availability of critical services in the event of disruptions. It ensures the maintenance or recovery of operations, including services to customers, when confronted with adverse events such as natural disasters, technological failures, human error, or terrorism.
